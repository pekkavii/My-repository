Nuclear Power Plant Accident Model

This model illustrates the potential consequences of a nuclear power plant accident based on wind conditions and INES class. It shows the radiation dose accumulated over one week by an individual remaining in the affected area. The selected accident is entirely hypothetical, and the release magnitude follows the international INES scale classifications.

INES Classes

INES (International Nuclear Event Scale) describes nuclear event severity on a scale of 1–7:

  • 3–4: Minor risk to the environment
  • 5: Localised serious damage
  • 6: Wider environmental impact
  • 7: Major accident (e.g. Chernobyl, Fukushima)

Radiation Doses

  • 1–10 mSv: No significant health effects
  • 10–100 mSv: Small increased cancer risk
  • 100 mSv – 1 Sv: Growing risk of serious health effects
  • >1 Sv: Acute radiation syndrome likely; potentially fatal at higher doses

Important

This model is for illustrative purposes only. In a real emergency, always follow official authorities' instructions. Protective actions aim to keep population radiation exposure as low as possible and restore normal living conditions after a radiation emergency.
