Nuclear Power Plant Accident Model

This model illustrates the potential consequences of a nuclear power plant accident based on wind conditions and INES class. It shows the radiation dose accumulated over one week by an individual remaining in the affected area. The selected accident is entirely hypothetical, and the release magnitude follows the international INES scale classifications. If a hypothetical plant is selected for which the maximum consequences cannot reach the highest INES classes, that release is not shown.

INES Classes

INES (International Nuclear Event Scale) classes describe the severity of nuclear events on a scale of 1–7:

  • 3–4: Minor risk to the environment
  • 5: Localised serious damage
  • 6: Wider environmental impact
  • 7: Major accident (e.g. Chernobyl, Fukushima)

Radiation Doses

Radiation exposure may cause health effects:

  • 1–10 mSv: No significant health effects
  • 10–100 mSv: Small increased cancer risk
  • 100+ mSv: Growing risk of serious health effects

Important

This model is for illustrative purposes only. In a real emergency situation, always follow official authorities' instructions. Protective actions aim to keep population radiation exposure as low as possible, minimise other harmful effects, and restore normal living conditions and societal function after a radiation emergency.
